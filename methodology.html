Why This Exists

Administrative decisions are under-measured

Healthcare operators make hundreds of administrative decisions every week — when to send an authorization, how to fill a cancelled slot, when a claim is likely to be denied, whether a referral is likely to convert. Most of these decisions are made on instinct, not data.

The tools here are designed to change that. Each one takes a single operational question — the kind that gets answered informally a dozen times a day — and gives it a structured framework, a benchmark, and a quantified output. Not to replace operator judgment, but to sharpen it.

This project started with a simple observation: the gap between how well-run healthcare organizations operate and how average ones operate is almost entirely explained by administrative decisions, not clinical ones. The clinical care is often comparable. The operations rarely are.

Every tool published here is part of a larger attempt to document that gap — to give it numbers, benchmarks, and frameworks that any operator can apply without a consultant, a software license, or a six-month implementation.

The Framework

Six phases, one through-line

The tools are organized around a six-phase methodology adapted from enterprise solutions selling frameworks — specifically, the sequence of how complex operational problems actually get solved in practice. The phases run in order, but real organizations move between them continuously.

01
Listen

Understand what leadership, staff, and patients are actually experiencing before diagnosing anything. Surface the presenting problem without assuming you know the root cause.

02
Diagnose

Identify the real operational gap — not the symptom, but the system. Score current state against benchmarks across intake, auth, scheduling, revenue cycle, and workforce domains.

03
Recommend

Translate findings into prioritized, defensible recommendations. Model the ROI of different interventions and help operators understand their tradeoffs before committing resources.

04
Decide

Support the decision with the right artifacts — business cases, stakeholder frameworks, approval pathway maps. Get the right people to yes with confidence.

05
Execute

Implement with discipline. Scoping tools, sprint frameworks, go-live checklists, and HIPAA compliance guides for the build-out phase where most initiatives stall or succeed.

06
Operationalize

Sustain, measure, and improve. KPI dashboards, performance benchmarkers, and continuous improvement trackers for organizations that have implemented change and need to hold the gains.

Each tool in this library maps to one of these phases. The diagnostic tools (like IntakeIQ) live in phases 1–2. Calculators and ROI models live in phase 3. Implementation guides live in phases 4–5. Dashboards and benchmarkers live in phase 6.

About This Project

Built by someone who's done the work

These tools come from direct experience building and scaling healthcare operations — not theory. The benchmarks, scoring logic, and framework decisions here are drawn from real implementations, including an AI-assisted intake and authorization system that reduced patient wait times by 83% across a multi-state specialty care organization, recognized as a Gartner case study and featured at Appian World 2025.

The goal is to make practitioner-level operational knowledge freely available to operators who can use it — without a consulting engagement, a software license, or a conference ticket.
