What is a patient actually worth to your practice?

Patient lifetime value (LTV) is the single most important number most practices have never calculated. It determines what a referral is worth, what a no-show really costs, and whether an appeal is worth filing. This tool walks you through every variable.

What is Patient LTV — and why does it matter?
Patient LTV is the total net revenue a single patient generates over their entire care relationship with your practice. It's not just one session — it's every session, adjusted for attendance, reimbursement rate, and what you actually collect.
LTV = Sessions/Week × Weeks in Care × Rate/Session × Attendance Rate × Collection Rate
Most operators think of a cancelled appointment as a $150 loss. The reality is often 10–100x that, once you account for the full care episode. Knowing your LTV changes how you think about intake investment, referral relationships, no-show policy, and appeal decisions.
